# L-com HG2415Y-RSP – 2.4 GHz 15 dBi Yagi Antenna Dataset

**Product Overview**  
- **Model**: HG2415Y-RSP (HyperGain series)  
- **Type**: Radome-enclosed directional Yagi antenna  
- **Frequency Band**: 2.4 GHz ISM (WiFi / Bluetooth)  
- **Primary Applications**: IEEE 802.11b/g/n Wireless LAN, directional / multipoint links, Bluetooth, public WiFi hotspots, point-to-point or point-to-multipoint setups  
- **Key Features**:  
  - High gain with narrow 30° beamwidth  
  - All-weather radome (UV-stable, UL flame-rated)  
  - DC short lightning protection  
  - Flexible polarization (vertical or horizontal)  
  - Includes tilt-and-swivel mast mount  
  - Optional wall/ceiling mounts available (e.g., HGX-UMOUNT02/03)

### Radiation Pattern Notes (from 2D Datasheet)

- **Azimuth / Elevation Patterns**: Standard Yagi patterns provided in the PDF (likely E-plane and H-plane plots)  
  - Narrow main lobe (~30° HPBW) for high directivity  
  - Strong front-to-back ratio (typical for 15 dBi Yagi: >20–25 dB, though not explicitly quantified in text)  
  - Low sidelobes due to classic multi-element Yagi design (reflector + driven + multiple directors)  
  - Patterns show excellent forward gain concentration with minimal back radiation  

### Additional Notes

- **Variants**: The HG2415Y series includes connector options (e.g., N-Female: HG2415Y-NF, N-Male: HG2415Y-NM, RP-SMA variants). The -RSP specifically uses a 4 ft RP-SMA Plug pigtail for direct connection to WiFi radios/devices.  
- **Construction**: Enclosed in protective radome for outdoor / harsh environments (unlike open-element Yagis).  
- **Performance Context**: Achieves ~15 dBi gain in a compact, rugged form factor suitable for long-range 2.4 GHz links (e.g., extending WiFi coverage in rural/hotspot scenarios).
